In this work, we explore the intricate relationship between the Anti-de Sitter/Conformal Field Theory (AdS/CFT) correspondence and Light-Front Quantum Chromodynamics (LFQCD). Our primary objective is to address the challenges associated with non-perturbative calculations in quantum field theories, particularly in the context of strong interactions among quarks and gluons. We demonstrate how the AdS/CFT framework can provide significant insights into the structure and dynamics of hadronic states, facilitating a deeper understanding of confinement and the nature of the strong force.  Employing a combination of holographic dualities and light-front dynamics, we establish a systematic methodology to translate problems in LFQCD into solvable scenarios within the AdS space. Through this approach, we derive analytical expressions for hadron wave functions and examine their implications for scattering processes. Our results reveal that the holographic framework not only preserves crucial symmetries of the original field theory but also simplifies the computational aspects traditionally regarded as intractable in standard LFQCD treatments.  Additionally, we present numerical simulations that illustrate the concordance between predictions made through our hybrid approach and empirical data from high-energy experiments. The insights garnered from this study pave the way for further applications of the AdS/CFT correspondence in light-front formalisms, driving advancements in theoretical research and phenomenological modeling in particle physics. Our findings underscore the potency of integrating diverse theoretical frameworks to enhance our understanding of QCD and its fundamental principles.
