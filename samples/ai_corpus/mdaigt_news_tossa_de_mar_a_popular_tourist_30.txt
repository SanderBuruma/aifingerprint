Tossa de Mar, a popular tourist destination in Spain, has introduced strict rules for parties and entertainment events to maintain peace and order in the town. Party organizers must obtain permits from the local authorities and submit their itineraries to the police. These measures aim to ensure that tourists do not "do whatever they want" during their stay, as stated by the mayor, Josep Lluis Sastre.  Under the new regulations, visitors could be fined up to €500 (around £480) if they create loud noises or disturbances that disturb other tourists or local residents. The rules apply year-round, not just during peak tourist season.  Sastre expressed his concern over the increasing number of noise complaints from both tourists and residents in recent years. "We want tourists to have a good time, but they must respect the community and the neighbours," he said. The mayor also urged party organizers to be responsible and to avoid activities that could disrupt the peace and harmony of the town.  The new regulations have been welcomed by many residents, who have long complained about late-night parties and loud music. However, some tourists have expressed disappointment, arguing that they came to Tossa de Mar to let loose and enjoy themselves. Sastre acknowledged the issue and advised tourists to plan their activities accordingly, adding that there are plenty of other places in the Costa Brava where they can party without restrictions.  Despite some initial resistance, the new rules are expected to help reduce noise complaints and maintain a peaceful atmosphere in Tossa de Mar. The town's authorities will closely monitor the situation and adjust the regulations as necessary to ensure the safety and enjoyment of all visitors.
