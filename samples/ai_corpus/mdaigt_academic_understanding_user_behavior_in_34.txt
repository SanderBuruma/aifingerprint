Understanding user behavior in smartphone applications is crucial for personalized services and marketing strategies. This study aims to explore the effectiveness of feature discovery for predicting app usage on smartphones. The methodology involves collecting usage data from various apps on users' devices and analyzing features such as time of day, app category, and duration of use. Machine learning algorithms are employed to model user behavior and predict future app usage patterns. The key findings reveal that certain features, such as time of day and app category, significantly impact app usage prediction accuracy. Moreover, the study demonstrates that incorporating feature discovery techniques enhances the predictive performance compared to baseline models. Overall, this research contributes to the field of app analytics by providing insights into user behavior and emphasizing the importance of feature engineering for accurate app usage prediction.
