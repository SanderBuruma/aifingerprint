In this study, we investigate the dynamics and societal impacts of partisan content sharing on Facebook, a prevalent social media platform influencing political discourse. Our research aims to address the increasing polarization within online communities, stemming from the rapid dissemination of ideologically concordant information. By leveraging a dataset comprising public Facebook posts from diverse political affiliations, we apply a mixed-methods approach that combines quantitative analysis with qualitative content examination. This methodology enables us to identify patterns of partisan content sharing and assess its broader societal implications.  Our findings reveal that partisan sharing is often characterized by sensationalized narratives that reinforce existing ideological biases, thereby exacerbating echo chamber effects. We observe significant correlations between high-frequency sharing of partisan content and increased divisiveness among users, contributing to a fragmented public sphere. Moreover, the prevalence of false or misleading information in these shared posts raises concerns about the quality of political information consumed on the platform.  Importantly, our research highlights the role of Facebook's algorithmic design in amplifying polarized content, suggesting that platform policies significantly influence sharing behaviors. We propose actionable recommendations for social media platforms, including enhancing transparency in content curation algorithms and promoting diversity in user news feeds, to mitigate the adverse consequences of partisan sharing. Our study advances understanding of the nexus between social media use and political polarization, offering insights for policymakers and technology developers aiming to foster healthier online political engagement. Through this work, we contribute to the ongoing discourse on the responsibilities of social media platforms in sustaining democratic processes and societal cohesion.
