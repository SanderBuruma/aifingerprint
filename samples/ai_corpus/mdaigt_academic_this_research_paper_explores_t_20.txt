This research paper explores the application of numerical relativity to D-dimensional axially symmetric spacetimes. The objective of the study is to develop a formalism suitable for analyzing such complex spacetimes and to test the efficiency and accuracy of codes designed for this purpose. The methodology involves adapting numerical relativity techniques to account for the higher dimensionality and axial symmetry of the spacetimes under investigation. Additionally, code testing is conducted to validate the numerical algorithms and ensure their reliability in capturing the dynamics of these systems. Key findings include the successful implementation of the formalism for D-dimensional axially symmetric spacetimes and the verification of the code's ability to accurately model the complex gravitational interactions in these higher-dimensional settings. The results from the code tests demonstrate the robustness and effectiveness of the numerical techniques employed in this study. In conclusion, this research contributes to the advancement of numerical relativity in higher dimensions and provides a solid foundation for future investigations into the dynamics of axially symmetric spacetimes in D dimensions.
