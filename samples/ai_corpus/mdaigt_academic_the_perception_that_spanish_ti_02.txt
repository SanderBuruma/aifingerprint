The perception that Spanish timekeeping is irregular and flexible is a long-standing stereotype. However, empirical evidence on the matter is scarce. This study investigates the degree to which Spanish time schedules deviate from traditional schedules, comparing them to those in Italy and the United Kingdom. Data was collected through surveys administered to residents of Madrid, Rome, and London. The results indicate that, despite the stereotype, Spanish schedules are not more irregular than those in Italy or the United Kingdom. The findings also reveal that cultural values and habits contribute to the perception of flexible timekeeping in Spain. The research suggests that the stereotype of the "late Spanish" originated from historical and social factors, rather than objective timekeeping practices. Overall, the study challenges the prevalent assumption that Spanish schedules are significantly more irregular than those in other Western European countries.
