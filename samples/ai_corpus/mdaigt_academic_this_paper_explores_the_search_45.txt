This paper explores the search for natural and split supersymmetry in proton-proton collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 13 TeV, focusing on final states with jets and missing transverse momentum. The analysis is based on data collected by the CMS experiment at the Large Hadron Collider. In the context of the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM), the study sets limits on the masses of supersymmetric particles using various search strategies, including the use of multijet final states, and the optimization of event selection criteria. The study also employs a split supersymmetry scenario, where the supersymmetric partners of the electroweak gauginos and the scalar top quarks have significantly different masses. In this scenario, the study sets strong limits on the masses of the heavier partners, improving previous results. The investigation also includes the assessment of the implications of the results on the overall MSSM parameter space and potential future directions for supersymmetry searches at the LHC.
