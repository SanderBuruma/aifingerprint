In this study, we investigate the age and binarity of Fomalhaut, employing multi-wavelength photometric data and theoretical models to refine our understanding of its stellar properties. Our analysis reveals that Fomalhaut is approximately 430 million years old, consistent with previous studies but with improved precision through the use of higher-resolution observations. We also conducted a comprehensive binary companion search using interferometric techniques, which indicated no significant secondary component within a projected separation of 1.5 arcseconds. This result supports the hypothesis that Fomalhaut is a single star, resolving a long-standing debate about its potential companions. The methodologies employed include high-cadence photometry across multiple wavelengths, adaptive optics imaging, and interferometric observations to achieve these findings. Our work contributes to the broader context of stellar evolution and the formation scenarios of stars in our solar neighborhood.
