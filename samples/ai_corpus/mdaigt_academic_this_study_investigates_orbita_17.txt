This study investigates orbital phase-dependent gamma-ray emissions from the black widow pulsar, Vela X-1. Through detailed analysis of archival Fermi-LAT data, we identify distinct gamma-ray flux variations that correlate with the orbital phase of the binary system. These variations suggest a mechanism where the gamma-ray emission is modulated by the interaction between the relativistic wind from the neutron star and the surrounding pulsar wind nebula. Our findings indicate that the gamma-ray emission is significantly enhanced during the ascending phase of the orbit, when the relativistic wind is more efficiently coupled to the nebula. This research provides evidence for the orbital modulation of gamma-ray emissions in black widow systems, offering insights into the complex dynamics of these unique stellar binaries. The methodology involves spectral analysis of gamma-ray data and modeling of the nebular structure to interpret the observed phase-dependent variability.
