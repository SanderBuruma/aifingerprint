Understanding the effects of variable thermal diffusivity on convection dynamics is essential for multiple scientific applications, from climate modeling to industrial processes. This study aims to investigate how variations in thermal diffusivity influence the structural characteristics and stability of convective flows. A comprehensive numerical simulation approach was employed, utilizing a modified Navier-Stokes equation to account for spatially varying thermal diffusivity. The simulations were conducted under controlled temperature gradients to simulate various indicative scenarios of natural convection. Key findings reveal that increased variability in thermal diffusivity significantly alters convection patterns, leading to both intensified and weakened flow structures. Notably, regions of high diffusivity exhibited enhanced thermal stratification, while low diffusivity facilitated rapid mixing of fluid layers. The analysis also uncovered that the transition threshold for convective onset shifts in response to changes in diffusivity, indicating a critical relationship between thermal properties and thermal convection stability. The results offer valuable insights into the design of thermal systems and enhance our understanding of natural phenomena, such as oceanic currents and atmospheric convection. In conclusion, recognizing and incorporating the effects of variable thermal diffusivity is critical for accurate predictions of convective behavior in various applications, suggesting directions for future research to further explore its implications under different environmental conditions.
