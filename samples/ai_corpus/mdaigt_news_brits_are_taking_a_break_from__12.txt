Brits are taking a break from their digital lives, with many opting for rural getaways in a bid to disconnect from the world and recharge. However, it appears that some are struggling to resist the temptation of checking work emails, even on holiday.  According to a recent survey, half of Brits admit to checking work emails while on holiday, with many feeling the need to stay on top of their work despite being on break. A third of those surveyed regret spending so much time on their work emails, with some feeling that they have missed out on precious time to enjoy the sights and scenery.  The trend towards digital detox is gaining momentum, with many people seeking out rural retreats that offer a chance to leave their smartphones and tablets firmly switched off. These getaways often take place in areas with no signal and no Wi-Fi, providing the perfect opportunity to disconnect from the digital world and reconnect with nature.  For those who are looking to take a break from the digital grind, these rural retreats offer a chance to unwind and recharge. Without the constant distraction of social media and email notifications, people can focus on enjoying the beauty of their surroundings and taking time to relax.  While some may see the digital detox movement as a threat to productivity, many others are finding that it has helped them to be more present and mindful in their daily lives. By taking a break from the digital world, people can gain a fresh perspective and come back to their work with renewed energy and focus.  As the digital detox movement continues to gain momentum, it is clear that many Brits are looking for ways to disconnect from the digital world and reconnect with nature. Whether it is a weekend getaway or a longer holiday, taking a break from the digital life can have a profound impact on both physical and mental health.
