Advancements in nuclear structure theory have underscored the necessity of accurately modeling the dipole response of open-shell nuclei, particularly under relativistic frameworks. This study aims to explore the application of the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQPTBA) to better understand the dipole excitations in these complex systems. Utilizing a combination of nuclear mean-field theory and linear response theory, we employ RQPTBA to investigate the dipole response characteristics of a selection of open-shell nuclei, including carbon-13 and oxygen-17. Our methodology integrates the relativistic effects inherent in the quasiparticle framework, allowing for the systematic description of both particle-hole excitations and their interactions within the nuclear medium. Key findings reveal that the inclusion of relativistic effects significantly alters the predicted energy spectra and transition strengths of dipole modes when compared to non-relativistic approaches. Notably, our results indicate an enhanced response in certain open-shell configurations, attributed to the interplay between single-particle states and collective excitations. Additionally, we observe a noteworthy sensitivity to the underlying mean-field structure, emphasizing the importance of precise nuclear interactions in modeling the dipole response. Ultimately, this study elucidates the efficacy of RQPTBA in capturing the intricacies of open-shell nuclei, providing a comprehensive framework for future explorations of nuclear dynamics and collective phenomena, thereby contributing to the broader understanding of nuclear excitations in relativistic contexts.
