In this study, we investigate the behavior of excited random walks (ERW) on one-dimensional lattice structures with Markovian cookie stacking rules. ERW is a non-equilibrium statistical physics model characterized by a random walker that moves according to a biased probability distribution and experiences energy excitations upon visits to specific lattice sites. The Markovian cookie stacking rules represent a stochastic process in which the cookie stack heights on the lattice sites are updated based on the random walker's position and current stack height. Our numerical simulations reveal that the presence of cookie stacking leads to intriguing dynamical behaviors in the ERW model. Specifically, we observe the emergence of power-law distributed waiting times between consecutive jumps, with an exponent that depends on the cookie stacking rates. Furthermore, we find that the average jump distance scales with the system size as a power law, and the scaling exponent is also influenced by the cookie stacking rates. We also provide an analytical framework to describe the dynamical behaviors observed in our simulations. By mapping the ERW model onto a continuum field theory, we derive a Fokker-Planck equation that governs the probability density function of the random walker's position and stack height. We use this equation to calculate the expected waiting times and average jump distances, and we find good agreement with our simulation results. Our study sheds light on the complex interplay between non-equilibrium dynamics and stochastic processes in statistical physics models. The results obtained from the ERW model with Markovian cookie stacking rules have potential applications in various areas, such as population dynamics, transport phenomena, and information processing.
