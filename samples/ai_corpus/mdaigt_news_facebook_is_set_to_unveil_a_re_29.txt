Facebook is set to unveil a redesigned News Feed at an event scheduled for Thursday morning. The social media giant's News Feed, which serves as the primary content stream on the homepage for hundreds of millions of users, hasn't undergone a major overhaul since 2009.  The new design, which has been in testing for several months, aims to make the News Feed more visually appealing and easier to navigate. According to sources familiar with the matter, the revamped layout places a greater emphasis on photos and videos, with larger images taking up more real estate on the page. Text posts will be displayed in a more compact format to accommodate the bigger visual elements.  Another notable change is the introduction of a new sidebar, which Facebook has dubbed the "Explore Feed." This feature is designed to help users discover new content beyond what's in their main News Feed. The Explore Feed will reportedly include content from Pages that users have liked, as well as suggested content based on their interests and interactions on the platform.  In addition, Facebook is said to be rolling out several new tools to help users manage their News Feeds more effectively. These tools include the ability to hide certain types of posts, such as those with too many photos or videos, and the ability to unfollow multiple posts at once.  The redesigned News Feed comes as Facebook continues to face intense competition from other social media platforms, particularly in the visual content space. Instagram, which is owned by Facebook, has seen meteoric growth in recent years, making it a formidable rival in the world of image and video sharing.  The update is expected to roll out to all users in the coming weeks, with some users already reporting that they have been given access to the new design. Facebook's event, which is scheduled to begin at 1000 AM PT, is being livestreamed on the company's website and Facebook Page. The event will be led by Facebook's chief product officer, Chris Cox, who is expected to provide more details about the redesigned News Feed and other product announcements.
