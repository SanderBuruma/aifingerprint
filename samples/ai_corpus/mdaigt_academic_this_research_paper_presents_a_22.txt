This research paper presents AKARI observations of brown dwarfs focusing on the detection and analysis of CO and CO₂ bands in the near-infrared spectra. The primary objective of this study is to investigate the presence and characteristics of these molecules in the atmospheres of brown dwarfs using data from the AKARI space telescope. The methodology involves analyzing the near-infrared spectra obtained by AKARI to identify and study the specific absorption features of CO and CO₂.   Key findings reveal the presence of prominent CO and CO₂ bands in the spectra of the observed brown dwarfs, indicating the presence of these molecules in their atmospheres. The strengths and positions of these bands provide insights into the atmospheric composition, temperature, and chemistry of these substellar objects. This study contributes to a better understanding of the atmospheric properties and chemical processes occurring in brown dwarfs, bridging the gap between gas giant planets and low-mass stars.  In conclusion, the detection and analysis of CO and CO₂ bands in the near-infrared spectra of brown dwarfs using AKARI observations offer valuable information about the atmospheric composition and physical conditions of these intriguing celestial objects. The results of this study have implications for our understanding of brown dwarfs and provide a basis for further research in exoplanet atmospheres and substellar objects.
