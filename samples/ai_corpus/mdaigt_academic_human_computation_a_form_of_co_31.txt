Human computation, a form of collaborative problem-solving, has gained significant attention in recent years as a means of engaging citizens in scientific research. Citizen science projects, in particular, rely heavily on human computation to analyze and process large datasets. However, understanding the factors that contribute to volunteers' engagement in these projects is crucial for their success. This study aimed to identify the engagement profiles of volunteers in human computation for citizen science projects, with a focus on the psychological, social, and environmental factors that influence their participation.  A mixed-methods approach was employed, combining both qualitative and quantitative data collection and analysis methods. A survey of 150 volunteers participating in human computation tasks was conducted, supplemented by in-depth interviews with 30 participants. The data was analyzed using thematic analysis and statistical models to identify patterns and correlations between engagement factors and volunteer behavior.  The results revealed that volunteers' engagement profiles can be categorized into three distinct groups the 'Motivated', 'Curious', and 'Social'. The 'Motivated' group was characterized by a strong sense of purpose and intrinsic motivation, while the 'Curious' group was driven by a desire for knowledge and understanding. The 'Social' group, on the other hand, was influenced by social norms and peer pressure. Environmental factors, such as task complexity and feedback mechanisms, also played a significant role in shaping volunteers' engagement.  The findings of this study have implications for the design and implementation of citizen science projects, highlighting the importance of tailoring engagement strategies to the diverse needs and motivations of volunteers. By understanding the engagement profiles of volunteers, researchers and practitioners can develop more effective strategies for increasing participation, retention, and overall success in human computation for citizen science projects.
